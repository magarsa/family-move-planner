## FHA Loan — Likely Not an Option Here

FHA loan limits for the Charlotte-Concord-Gastonia MSA (both NC and SC sides) are in the **standard range around $498K–$524K** for 2025/2026 for a single-family home. With a $620K purchase:

- 10% down → $558K loan — **exceeds FHA limits**
- Even 3.5% (FHA minimum) → $598K loan — **way over**

FHA is off the table for a $620K home in Charlotte. You'd need a **conventional loan**.

## DTI Analysis — Will You Qualify?

Gross monthly income: **$173K / 12 = ~$14,417/mo**

The key question is whether lenders count the Des Moines mortgage against you. Most conventional lenders (Fannie/Freddie guidelines) allow you to **offset the existing mortgage with 75% of documented rental income**.

If Des Moines rents for ~$1,900–2,100/mo (realistic for a $340K home there):
- 75% rental credit: ~$1,425–$1,575
- Net Des Moines contribution to DTI: ~$325–475/mo

| Debt | Monthly |
|---|---|
| Charlotte PITI + PMI | ~$4,788 |
| Des Moines net (after rental offset) | ~$400 |
| Other debts (cars, student loans, etc.) | ~$TBD |
| **Estimated total** | **~$5,200+ before other debts** |

DTI at ~$5,200 / $14,417 = **~36%** — that's within the conventional 43–45% threshold, leaving some room for car payments and other debts. **You likely qualify**, but it's tight and depends heavily on your existing debt load.

⚠️ Lenders will also require **proof of rental income** — typically a signed lease agreement or 2 years of rental history on tax returns. If you haven't rented the house yet, some lenders will still use 75% of a market rent appraisal (Form 1007).

## Pros & Cons

**Pros of keeping and renting Des Moines:**
- Retain an appreciating asset in a stable Midwest market
- Tenant builds equity for you over time
- Potential tax deductions (depreciation, mortgage interest, repairs, management fees)
- Diversified real estate exposure in two markets
- Avoids selling in a potentially suboptimal timing

**Cons:**
- Likely cash flow negative short-term (~$300–500/mo out of pocket)
- Landlord responsibilities from 1,000 miles away — you'll need a property manager
- Ties up capital that could be used for a bigger Charlotte down payment
- Adds DTI pressure; lenders scrutinize this carefully
- Vacancy, bad tenants, or major repairs can hurt fast
- Two mortgages = more financial exposure if income drops

**Pros of selling Des Moines instead:**
- Frees up equity (likely $50K–$100K+ if you have any) toward a larger Charlotte down payment
- Eliminates PMI on Charlotte home if you can hit 20%
- Cleaner DTI — easier qualification
- Zero landlord headaches

---

## Bottom Line

You **probably qualify** for a conventional loan on the Charlotte home while retaining Des Moines as a rental, assuming your other debts are reasonable. But it'll be tight and cash-flow negative on Des Moines for a while.

The bigger question is: **how much equity do you currently have in Des Moines?** If you're sitting on $60–80K in equity, selling and rolling that into Charlotte could:
- Get you to 20% down → no PMI → saves ~$280/mo
- Lower the Charlotte loan to ~$496K → meaningfully lower payment
- Eliminate the rental management headache entirely

That path might be financially cleaner. Worth running both scenarios with a mortgage broker who does investment property loans.
